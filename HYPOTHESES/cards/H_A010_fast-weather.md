# 🜂 H_A010 — Fast weather ("빠른 풍화")

## Concept

Spread finely-milled olivine/basalt across farmland and coasts. Natural silicate weathering
(`Mg₂SiO₄ + CO₂ → MgCO₃ + SiO₂`) pulls CO₂ into **stable carbonate permanently** — accelerate a
geological process that already regulates Earth's climate over megayears, with **no plant, no
air-handling wall (H_013), and permanence by chemistry**.

## Falsifiable prediction (kernel — not run)

Olivine can fix ~1 ton CO₂ per ~1.25 tons rock (stoichiometric ceiling). The rate is **grain-size /
dissolution-limited** (years), and the milling-to-fine-powder energy is the **net-negativity check
(cf. H_014)**: predict ~0.1–0.3 ton CO₂ emitted per ton captured for milling+transport on a clean
grid (NET-positive), more on a dirty one (could go futile). Kill criterion: dissolution rate too slow
(decadal) and milling energy eats the benefit. Vindicated if fine-grain weathering + clean energy nets
≫ 0 within years.

## Why novel (vs the verified H_0xx chain)

Uses the planet's own carbon thermostat sped up — a geochemical axis orthogonal to every engineered
level; storage and capture are the same irreversible reaction.

**🟢 SUPPORTED** — graduated by running the kernel. Milling olivine (~300 kWh/ton) is small vs capture,
