# 🜂 H_A007 — Breathing stone ("숨쉬는 콘크리트")

## Concept

Embed carbonic anhydrase + cyanobacteria in a hydrogel-mineral matrix so ordinary **walls, roads, and
facades passively fix CO₂ into carbonate** using sunlight and ambient air. No dedicated plants — the
built environment's enormous existing surface area becomes the contactor, sidestepping CAPEX (H_004)
and the air-handling wall (H_013, the air comes to the city for free).

## Falsifiable prediction (kernel — not run)

Carbonic anhydrase turnover ≈ 10⁶ /s/enzyme, but the **areal rate is CO₂-mass-transfer-limited**, not
enzyme-limited. Predict an areal fixation of ~order 10–100 g CO₂/m²/day (cf. fast-growing biofilms).
Global urban surface ~10¹² m² → ~3–30 Gt/yr ceiling IF every surface participated — same order as the
H_009 target. **Kill criterion: mineralization saturates the surface (carbonate crust blocks further
uptake) and material durability** — predict it caps at a thin surface layer unless self-renewing.

## Why novel (vs the verified H_0xx chain)

Distributes capture into infrastructure that already exists — no L4 plant, no L2 reactor. The contactor
is the city. Orthogonal to the entire engineered-plant stack.
