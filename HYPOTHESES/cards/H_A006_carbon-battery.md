# 🜂 H_A006 — Carbon battery ("탄소 배터리")

## Concept

A redox-active quinone electrode binds CO₂ when **charged** and releases it when **discharged** —
so the same cell that captures carbon also stores energy. Charge it from curtailed renewables and the
grid's surplus literally becomes captured carbon. **No temperature or pressure swing — it removes the
dominant H_010 sensible-heat term electrochemically.**

## Falsifiable prediction (kernel — not run)

Energy ≈ (electrons per CO₂) × (cell voltage) × F. At 1–2 e⁻/CO₂ and ~0.5–1.0 V: ≈ **48–193 kJ/mol**
— comparable to today's TSA (H_010) but NOT below the 19.3 kJ/mol floor, and it sidesteps the
sensible-heat penalty entirely. **Predict: lands in the amine-TSA energy band but with electrical
(exergy-clean, renewable-couplable) energy and zero thermal mass** — the live advantage is *quality
of energy + grid coupling*, not a lower absolute number. Vindicated if cycle life × current density
make it cheaper per ton than TSA.

## Why novel (vs the verified H_0xx chain)

H_019 prescribed "remove the sensible-heat term"; this is a concrete electrochemical route that ALSO
fuses capture with grid storage — a dual-use no level of the spec proposes. Closest to real (cf. MIT
electro-swing) yet SF-extended to planetary "carbon batteries."
